FUN FACT ABOUT DINUGUAN

Dinuguan gets its name from the word ``dugo,'' which means blood. Traditionally,
the dish is made with pork intestines, liver, kidneys, and lungs and served with puto, a steamed rice cake.
Oftentimes, dinuguan and lechon, a whole pig roasted on a spit over a charcoal or wood fire, are served together at fiestas.
